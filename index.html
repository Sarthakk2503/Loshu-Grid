History

Lo shu grid is a square-shaped grid with 3 rows and 3 columns. 1 to 9 numbers are placed in the 3 rows and 3 columns of the Loso grid. Numbers are arranged in such a way that by adding any three numbers: horizontally, vertically or diagonally, the sum equals “15.” Lo shu grid is also known as Magic or Sacred Square in Chinese Numerology.

Lo shu grid is a part of Chinese Numerology. The origin of the Lou shu grid is about 4000 years old. There was a Hwang-Ho river in China that used to experience floods regularly. People in the nearby areas were troubled because of the devastating floods. They used to think it was a result of a curse by God/Goddess. The mythical Emperor of Wu-of-Hsia was determined to find a way to control the floods. One day, he passed by the river and saw a shell of a tortoise in the river. On the shell, a 3*3 grid was engraved. The perfect 3*3 square came to be called as Lu shu grid or the Lo shu grid.

Horizontal planes in Loshu Grid

Mind Plane

This is the topmost horizontal line of the birth chart. The Mind Plane represents the innate thinking capacity of an individual. It reflects the powers of memory, intellect, analyzing capability, and thought.It relates to career and communication

Soul Plane

The middle horizontal line of the birth chart is the emotional or the soul plane. It reflects sensitivity, freedom, love, artistic inclination, spiritual independence and intuition.It relates to love and marriage of someone's life

Practical Plane

The bottom horizontal line of the birth chart is the practical plane. This plane represents practical abilities, organizational skills, materialistic abilities, verbal expression and the ability to do physical specially with one’s hand

Vertical Planes in Loshu Grid

Thought Plane

This is known as ‘The Line or Plane of Thought’. It reflects the ability of a person to think about new ideas. It also reflects the person’s love of order and method. This plane is therefore even referred to as ‘The Plane of the Planner’.

Will Plane

This is called ‘The Line of Plane of Determination and Will’. This vertical row reflects a person’s determination and will to succeed. It also shows a person’s patience and persistence in realizing his goals.It describes humanity and ambitions

Action Plane

This is called ‘The Line of Plane of Action’. It revolves around the individual’s ability to put his ideas and ability in action and execute them. This plane is therefore also known as The Plane of Action or The Plane of Activity.
